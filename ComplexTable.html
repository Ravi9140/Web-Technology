Camelids

The four llamas and two camels are camelids. Camelids tend to be larger and are strictly herbivorous. Camels differ from ruminants in several ways. They have a three-chambered rather than a four-chambered digestive tract; an upper that is split in two with each part separately mobile; and an isolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have long legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still.(from Wikipedia)
